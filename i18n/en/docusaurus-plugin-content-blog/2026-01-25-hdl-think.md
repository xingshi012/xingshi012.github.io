>I packed up my computer today and saw an article about the Contra game written by a high school student. I felt a little touched. Let’s write something.
<!-- truncate -->
I suddenly realized that I haven’t played games for a long time. Since I started working, it’s probably because I’m busy at work. When I have free time during the workday, I will go to Bilibili to watch videos, browse Tiktok, or read WeChat public account articles. During my free days, I have been working on my own blog website or visiting relatives' houses. Now there are no games on my mobile phone, let alone the computer. I remember that the only game left was when I was in college. The more popular hybrid version of Plants vs. Zombies. I haven’t paid attention to it for a long time. The numerical value is weird. It’s hard to pass every level without cheating. It’s different from the previous classic version of Plants vs. Zombies.

When I saw that article, I remembered that the college entrance examination was about to take place. In order to "kick Internet addiction" to prepare for the college entrance examination, I uninstalled all the games on my mobile phone at that time. In fact, it was nothing. After all, in high school, to be honest, I didn't study seriously. Afterwards, I took the college entrance examination. It was natural. But now that I can work and be engaged in the industry I like, I am a little lucky.

The game "Contra: Return" probably originated in 2017. I played Contra when I was a child. I remember that I had a game CD at home and plugged in the game controller to play it. Later, when a mobile game came out, I quickly registered it. It is probably the game I have played for the longest time. It was a nurturing game. I was busy with school at the time. I didn’t have much time to play. I logged in and played for some time during the winter and summer vacations of junior high school and high school. I think I should really pick it up when I go to college. I am no longer so busy with studies, and I have time to do tasks every day. I also received my favorite weapons, Shenglong and Dragon Cannon, but my fate with it ended there.

I remember that when I went to Qingdao in May 24, I deleted the game because my mobile phone had no memory to take photos. It has been almost two years in the blink of an eye. In fact, there is still a lot of reluctance to let go. I remember the Xinghai Competition on Monday night at 8 o'clock, the legion competition on Tuesday and Thursday, the "Fighting Aliens" on Wednesday, etc. I have almost forgotten the specific legion tasks, but I remember that in addition to going to class every day, Basically, I was tinkering with Contra. I really love this game, but after I got a little money in my pocket, I discovered that the outside world is actually much more interesting than the mobile phone. I can encounter all kinds of interesting things, meet all kinds of people, and see different beautiful scenery in different places. Suddenly I realized that the saying "the world is so big, I want to see it" seems to be reasonable. This virtual world can certainly kill time and bring fun, but how can the people or things encountered in the real world not be fun?

In fact, I have been working at this job for more than a year. To be honest, my hobbies seem to be limited to walking around on my days off and doing things I like and want to do. Or should I cultivate a meaningful interest? Of course, coding is one of them. I hope to continue to maintain it, and then write more interesting and meaningful articles to broaden my technology stack.

The fate of gaming is probably close, after all, I haven’t played gaming for more than a year.
